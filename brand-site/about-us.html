Our Story

A designer and an engineer walk into a workshop.
Lighting gets interesting.

Founded in 2013 in Minneapolis, Dutton Brown makes color-forward, made-to-order lighting and hardware for people who believe their spaces should have personality.

Where It Started

One fixture on Etsy. One really good idea.

In 2013, Zach Dutton and Thomas Brown had a hunch: the lighting world was overdue for some personality. They designed their first fixture, listed it on Etsy, and waited. People noticed. Designers noticed. The orders kept coming.

That single listing became a studio. The studio became a catalog of over 200 customizable products. But the idea never changed — lighting should never be boring, and neither should the people who make it.

Today, every fixture is still designed in-house, made to order, and shipped from our workshop in Minneapolis. We've gotten bigger, but we haven't gotten generic.

Lighting should never be boring — and neither should the company that makes it.
The idea behind everything we do

The Founders

One designs it. One builds it.
Both obsess over every detail.

Zach Dutton
Designer & Co-Founder

Zach brings the eye. With a background in industrial design, he's the one who decides what Dutton Brown looks like — the shapes, the colors, the details most people don't notice but would miss if they were gone. He believes good design should be confident, not careful.

Thomas Brown
Engineer & Co-Founder

Thomas makes it work. He engineered the production systems, built the customization platform, and figured out how to offer made-to-order at scale without cutting corners. If Zach dreams it, Thomas finds a way to ship it — correctly, on time, and built to last.

Milestones

From Etsy shop to design studio

2013

The First Fixture

Listed our first handcrafted light on Etsy. It sold. Then it sold again. We took the hint.

2015

Catalog Expansion

Grew from a handful of fixtures to a proper product line — pendants, sconces, and flush mounts.

2017

The Color System

Introduced our 13 signature powder coat colors. Color became our calling card.

2019

Hardware Collection

Launched cabinet pulls and hardware to complement our lighting — same colors, same craft.

2021

Trade Program

Formalized our designer and architect partnerships with dedicated support, samples, and specs.

Now

200+ Products & Growing

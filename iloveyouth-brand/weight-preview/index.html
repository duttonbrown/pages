Creative directions

Explorations — for consideration, not committed

A — Blue period + blue i-tittle

Regular 400 body, period and i-tittle both in brand blue. The two accent marks rhyme — same color, different positions — without changing letter weight. Works well at any size.

B — Blue period + blue i-tittle, inverse

Same on dark — white letters, blue accents on both dots. Reads immediately as a designed system rather than a coincidence.

C — Weight contrast
iloveyouth.

Light body, bold period. The period becomes a full typographic statement. More editorial and assertive. Best at large sizes; contrast disappears small.

D — Tracked brandmark
ily.

Regular 400 with wide tracking. Feels architectural and premium — closer to a luxury fashion mark. Period gets breathing room in the tracking rhythm.

E — Vertical letterstack
i l y .

Reads as a monogram column. Works well as a narrow sidebar, box spine, or embossed edge.

F — Scale contrast
ily.

Period dominates. Bold as a packaging graphic or campaign element — not a logo replacement but a strong brand moment.

G — Suggested direction: Regular 400 + blue accent on both dots, both marks

Both marks at Regular 400. Blue lands on every dot — period and i-tittle — making it a repeatable system accent rather than a one-off flourish. This is the direction to take to Illustrator first.

Live Satoshi web font via Fontshare — needs internet to render. Letterforms will differ slightly from Illustrator-outlined SVGs once re-exported. Use this to pick a direction, then re-set and outline in Illustrator at the chosen weight.

Who We Are

Dutton Brown is a made-to-order lighting and hardware manufacturer based in Minneapolis. We design and sell approximately 275 products with up to 39 color and finish variants each, sold primarily through our Shopify storefront. We process approximately 500 orders per month and have a 10-person team across operations, production, and admin.

Everything we make is built to order. Nothing ships off a shelf.

Our Manufacturing Workflow

We have two distinct production paths that any MRP system must handle simultaneously:

Path 1 — Standard Finishes

Finishes: Brass, Nickel, Black

  • Parts pre-finished, kept in stock
  • Order in → straight to assembly
  • Fast, no batching required
  • Black is sometimes ordered pre-finished from vendor due to high volume
Path 2 — Custom Colors (Powder Coat)

Finishes: Coral, Navy, Orange, Sage, and 30+ others

  • Raw uncoated parts pulled from stock
  • Orders accumulate until enough of a color to batch
  • Powder coat run in-house by color
  • Coated parts returned to inventory
  • Waiting assembly MOs proceed
Critical requirement: The system must generate a consolidated pick list by color — showing all open orders waiting for powder coat, grouped by finish, with aggregate part quantities across all waiting MOs. This is our single most operationally important workflow and the primary thing we need to validate.

Why MRPeasy Made the Shortlist

We evaluated seven platforms — Fishbowl, Craftybase, NetSuite, Odoo, Cin7, inFlow, and Katana — before landing here. MRPeasy stood out for three reasons:

1. Matrix BOMs

Instead of 39 separate BOMs per product, one BOM template with a "Finish" parameter that resolves the correct parts automatically on every order. For our catalog this is the difference between a manageable implementation and a ~10,000-record data entry project.

Example — Aura Pendant (39 variants, 25 parts) Matrix BOM — one template: Parameter: Finish = {BR, NI, BL, CORAL, NAVY, ORANGE, SAGE...} Fixed parts (20 — same for all finishes): Socket E26 ×1, Nipple NI3 ×2, Set Screw SS1 ×4, Wire WR2 ×36"... Parametric parts (5 — resolved by Finish): Canopy → {BR→CA5BR, NI→CA5NI, BL→CA5BL, CORAL→CA5-CORAL...} Globe Cap → {BR→GC6BR, NI→GC6NI, BL→GC6BL, CORAL→GC6-CORAL...} Lamp Cup → {BR→LC1BR, NI→LC1NI, BL→LC1BL, CORAL→LC1-CORAL...}

2. Right-Sized for Our Operation

Cloud-native, no server, no IT overhead. Shop Floor Kiosk means production workers don't need licenses. 2–3 licensed users covers our actual needs. Native Shopify, ShipStation, and QuickBooks integrations — not third-party plugins.

3. Automation-Ready

Full API access on Unlimited plan enables Make.com automations for real-time MO creation, powder coat pick lists, and Notion sync — which is our intended architecture.

What Success Looks Like

We're not looking for a sales pitch. We need to answer three questions before starting the trial:

  1. Powder coat pick list is buildable — either natively or via Make.com + API, we can generate a color-grouped, parts-aggregated pick list across all open orders waiting for powder coat.
  2. Matrix BOM works as documented — specifically for a product with 39 variants where some finishes are pre-stocked and some require custom powder coating, with the correct parts resolving automatically.
  3. Trial can be extended — 30 days is not enough to properly test with our catalog. We need confidence that we can run a real-world evaluation before committing.
If yes to all three, we start the trial immediately and target go-live by July 2026.
